Valse Sentimentale, Op. 51, No. 6

composer: Pyotr Illyich Tchaikovsky

Background information


"Valse sentimentale" translates to sentimental waltz.


This piece is the last of the Six Morceaux - Six Pieces piano solo written by Tchaikovsky on a commission in 1882. There are many different arrangements - the video references above is for an orchestra. Below is a piano solo played by Lucas Debargue at the International Tchaikovsky Competition in Moscow in 2015.


The full list of the Six Morceaux includes:

  1. Valse de salon
  2. Polka peu dansante
  3. Menuetto scherzoso
  4. Natha-Valse
  5. Romance
  6. Valse sentimentale

Each piece is dedicated to a woman - this particular piece is for Emma Genton.


In the image above displaying the first 7 bars, a waltz follows the beat "oom cha cha", played by the left hand on the piano. A graceful melody is played on the right hand, the top row. See here for a more indepth explanation.
