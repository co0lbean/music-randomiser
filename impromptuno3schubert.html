Impromptu no. 3

composer: Franz Schubert

Background information

An impromptu is a piece of music written in the spirit of improvisation, usually for a solo instrument. In this case, it is for the piano.

This gentle, somewhat melancholic piece is the third piece of the Four Impromptus, composed by Schubert in 1827, however, only the first two were published during his lifetime. Impromptu no. 3 was made public posthumously, and was published in G major instead of Gâ™­ major for many years.

Horowitz's performance made playing the piece look efforless - it seemed as though like his fingers were barely gliding on the keys, yet was able to make such beautiful sounds. Despite being short and sounding simple, it's regarded to be a hard piece to execute, with difficulties coming from getting the motifs and moods right.
