Background information

This particular version is played by Eric Lu, but you could check out Yuja Wang's, and Seong-Jin Cho's versions as well and compare them. Note the difference in tempo and expression - which do you prefer?


A Prelude is a short music piece - this is No.17 of the 24 Preludes. The Preludes cover all of the 24 keys cycle (12 major and 12 minor). Each piece written in the major key is followed by its relative minor. The order of which they were written and presented in the catalogue is annotated in blue. This is a rather interesting way to display the preludes.

Chopin himself has never played in public more than 4 preludes at once, and this trend continued for a few decades. Some say that they are meant to play continuously, whilst others suspect each is a short introductory, standalone piece for larger pieces. In modern times, the 24 Preludes have been performed in the correct sequence by big names such as Yuja Wang, lasting approximately 45 minutes in total.


Interpretation

"She told me, I love you" is the name given by the French pianist Alfred Cortot for this piece. Below is a short video from Fei Fei, interpreting the piece by its nickname.

Fei Fei describes the first two bars of repeated chords as the heart fluttering (annotated in blue), very gently expressed via piano ("p" circled in red). The first statement of the theme starts after that (indicated by first orange arrow), where it is continued to be played very softly - a woman telling a man that she loves him.

The second statement is played with forte, where perhaps it is used to describe the joy and realisation of the man, reciprocating his love for her.
